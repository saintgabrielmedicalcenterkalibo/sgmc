Orthopedics

Orthopedics helps patients by diagnosing, treating, and preventing problems related to the bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, and tendons. It provides care for conditions such as fractures, arthritis, sports injuries, spinal disorders, and congenital deformities. Orthopedic treatment may include medication, physical therapy, surgical procedures, or joint replacement to restore mobility and reduce pain.

Diagnostic

helps patients by identifying the cause of their illness or health problem accurately and quickly. Through tests like blood analysis, X-rays, MRIs, CT scans, and other examinations, diagnostic provides important information about the patient’s condition. This allows doctors to detect diseases early, choose the right treatment, and monitor the patient’s recovery.

Psychology

Psychology helps patients by improving their mental, emotional, and even physical well-being. It allows individuals to understand their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors more clearly, which helps them manage stress, anxiety, depression, and other mental health conditions. Through therapy and counseling, patients learn healthy coping strategies, problem-solving skills, and ways to regulate their emotions.

General Treatment

General treatment helps patients by addressing their physical health needs and promoting overall well-being. It includes medical care such as diagnosis, medication, minor procedures, and preventive measures like vaccinations and health check-ups. General treatment helps relieve symptoms, manage illnesses, and prevent complications, allowing the body to heal and function properly.

laboratory

Where tests are conducted out on clinical specimens to obtain information about the health of a patient to aid in diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disease. Clinical medical laboratories are an example of applied science, as opposed to research laboratories that focus on basic science, such as found in some academic institutions.

Medical laboratories vary in size and complexity and so offer a variety of testing services. More comprehensive services can be found in acute-care hospitals and medical centers, where 70% of clinical decisions are based on laboratory testing.

Hemodialysis

Healthy kidneys clean your blood and remove extra fluid in the form of urine. They also make substances that keep your body healthy. Dialysis replaces some of these functions when your kidneys no longer work. There are two different types of dialysis - hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis.

You need dialysis if your kidneys no longer remove enough wastes and fluid from your blood to keep you healthy. This usually happens when you have only 10 to 15 percent of your kidney function left. You may have symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, swelling and fatigue.

  • Follow a strict treatment schedule
  • Take medications regularly
  • Make changes in your diet

Nuclear Medicine

Nuclear medicine helps patients by diagnosing, monitoring, and sometimes treating disease using small amounts of radioactive materials (radiopharmaceuticals). These materials are generally safe and are designed to target specific organs or types of cells in the body.

Here’s how it helps patients:

  • Early & Accurate Diagnosis
  • Guides Treatment Decisions
  • Treatment of Certain Diseases
  • Safe & Minimally Invasive

Ultrasound

Ultrasound helps patients by using sound waves (not radiation) to create pictures of the inside of the body. It is safe, painless, and quick.

Here’s how it helps:

  • Diagnose Problems
  • Check Blood Flow
  • Guide Procedures
  • Safe for Babies and Children
